Topics of Interest

Relevant topics include, but are not limited to:

  • Diagnostic evaluation of robot manipulation
  • Fine-grained visuomotor perception and control
  • Vision-language-action models for manipulation
  • Semantic grounding and compositional task understanding
  • Robustness under visual, geometric, and linguistic shifts
  • Failure attribution and causal diagnosis
  • Precision, stability, and constraint-aware execution
  • Simulation-to-real evaluation and calibration
  • Multidimensional metrics and capability profiles
  • Benchmark design, reproducibility, and leaderboard reliability
  • Negative results and systematic failure analyses
  • Findings and methods arising from the MetaFine Open Competition

Participation in the MetaFine Open Competition is encouraged but not required for paper submission.

Submission Format

We welcome short research papers, position papers, benchmark proposals, competition reports, and reports of negative or preliminary results.

  • Length: 4 pages, excluding references and supplementary material
  • Format: CoRL 2026 paper template
  • Submission system: OpenReview
  • Review process: Double-blind

Previously published or accepted CoRL 2026 papers should not be resubmitted unless they contain substantial new material specifically developed for this workshop.

Review and Selection

Each submission will be reviewed by at least two members of the program committee. Papers will be assessed based on relevance to the workshop, technical soundness, clarity, originality of the perspective or evidence, and potential to stimulate discussion.

Accepted papers will be presented in an interactive poster session. A small number of submissions will be selected for spotlight presentations. Authors will also be encouraged to contribute their findings, failure cases, and open questions to the workshop’s breakout discussions and post-workshop community artifact.
